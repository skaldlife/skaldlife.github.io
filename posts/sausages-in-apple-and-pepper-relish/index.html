Sausages in apple and pepper relish

Posted Nov 2 2020-11-02T13:55:00+01:00 by Skald Life

Easy recipe for Sausages in apple and pepper relish

Autumn time comfort food

I can guarantee that you will enjoy these sausages in no time if you try and stick to the recipe. They are a perfect autumn comfort food especially if you would like to bring home some of the Oktoberfest atmosphere. I know this isn’t stricktly a Balkan recipe but Bavarian food has a strong foothold in my country, so I think I’m close enought to try and bring you a taste of this really easy recipe. These apple relish sausages are perfect dish for your next dinner party so let me know how it went for you.

I’ve been playing with this food for years and I updated the recipe over time but, but it still remained simple and easy to follow. Also as one cooking enthusiast to another, please feel free to add your flavors to this dish and share the results.

Ingredients

  • pork sausages of your choice, but they should be spicy
  • buns, if you manage to find something made of beer dough perfect
  • cheddar cheese
  • 1.7oz butter
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 larger onion
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 1 Granny Smith apple
  • 1 tablespoon od Dijon or Bavarian mustard
  • 1 table spoon of sour cream
  • 1 table spoon of finely chopped parsley

Directions

  1. Put olive oil and butter into a pan and let the butter melt.
  2. Cut the onion in half and cut it to thinner semicircles. Add it to the pan and fry it or about 5 minutes.
  3. Chop the apple and add it to the pan. Fry it for another 5 minutes.
  4. Then add chopped apple, mustard, cream and parsley. Mix everything for another five minutes.
  5. Turn of the heat, cover the pan and leave it on the side.
  6. Grill the sausages 3-4 minutes on the each side (or depending on which ones you bought).
  7. Cut the buns in half, grate the cheese over them and put them in an oven for a minute or two (356°F - 392°F). The cheese should melt but the buns should be slightly toasted
  8. Put the sausage on the bun, cover it with previously prepared relish, grab a beer and enjoy.
